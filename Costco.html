Costco App Redesign

This project was a conceptual redesign of the Costco app with a team of three designers. The project timeline was two weeks and I performed the role of interaction designer.

We were tasked with creating a Costco app that was designed around users. From the get-go it was clear that Costco had scaled their website down to fit a mobile screen. My first step was to complete a competitive analysis between the Costco, Target, Sam’s Club, and Amazon apps.

I listed out UI elements and took a close look at what tasks could be completed within each app.

During this time, my user researcher was completing interviews and creating a persona.

Based on the research, we came to the conclusion that the most effective purpose of the Costco app would be strictly for locating deals, coupons, and sales in order to draw more customers to the brick and mortar locations.

As a team, we completed a feature prioritization exercise in order to determine the features in our MVP. The result that we arrived at was that the app needed the ability to search for deals, browse deals by category, save deals for later, and locate deals at a specific store.

Based on the features we selected, I wrote three tasks to design the app interactions around.

Based on the features we selected, I wrote three tasks to design the app interactions around.

  1. Find a deal and save it for later use
  2. Sign up for a new membership
  3. Find the saved item from earlier and reveal the barcode for in-store checkout

Keeping the tasks in mind, I hosted a design studio as a team. We sketched out the home screen in order to rapidly ideate our UI elements and what features to put where. We then discussed our sketches and combined them into one master sketch incorporating everything we found was effective.

Using the results of the design studio, I started wireframing the screens in Sketch.

In order to make sure I wireframed every necessary screen, I wrote screen flows for each task. Each screen flow is a list of the steps that the user would go through to complete each task. By listing out these steps, I could see which screens they would be visiting and if there were different display states needed for any of these screens.

This quick and dirty wireframe served as the basis for the digital prototype.

The first step in turning the wireframe into a prototype was updating any necessary UI elements through the use of symbols.

Next I wrote task scenarios in collaboration with the user researcher, which provided specific details for each task.

Finally, I uploaded my screens to InVision and added hotspots for usability testing.

I also shared the prototype with the visual designer so that they could create screen mock-ups for our presentation.

After the user researcher had completed testing with a few people, we met as a team to discuss findings and simple solutions.
